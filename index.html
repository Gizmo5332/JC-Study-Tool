The Historical-Grammatical Approach
This method seeks to determine the meaning of the biblical text as originally understood by its writers — taking into account the historical context, grammatical meaning of the language, and cultural and literary setting in which it was written. The goal is to find the intended meaning of the text as it was originally communicated.

Manuscript Reliability
The New Testament has 5,600+ Greek manuscripts — more than any ancient writing. The earliest copy dates to within 100 years of the originals, with 99.5% textual accuracy. By comparison, Homer's Iliad has 643 copies with a 500-year gap. The NT also has 19,000+ copies in Syriac, Latin, Coptic, and Aramaic — total manuscript base exceeds 24,000.
